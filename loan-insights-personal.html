Personal Loan Knowledge Section

A Guide to Understanding Personal Loans

What is a Personal Loan?

A personal loan is an unsecured loan offered by banks or financial institutions based on a borrower's creditworthiness, used for personal expenses and repaid in fixed installments over a set period.

Conditions for Personal Loan

  • Credit Score: A good credit score is required to qualify for a personal loan.
  • Income Proof: Borrowers must provide proof of stable income (e.g., salary slips, bank statements).
  • Employment Status: Most lenders require borrowers to be employed (full-time or self-employed) with a steady income.
  • Age: Borrowers must be at least 18 years old, and usually under 60-65, depending on the lender.
  • Nationality/Residency: Borrowers must often be citizens or legal residents of the country where they are applying.
  • Loan Amount and Tenure: The loan amount and repayment period must fall within the lender’s prescribed limits.
  • Credit History: A clean or relatively clean credit history (with minimal defaults or bankruptcies) is preferred.

Required Documents for Personal Loan

  • Proof of Identity: Government-issued ID (e.g., Passport, Driver's License).
  • Proof of Address: Recent utility bill, rent agreement, or bank statement.
  • Proof of Income: Salary slips, bank statements, or income tax returns (ITR).
  • Credit Score Report: Credit report from a recognized credit bureau.
  • Employment Details: Employment verification letter or business proof (for self-employed).
  • Photographs: Passport-sized photographs (typically 2–3).
  • Bank Statements: Bank statements for the last 3–6 months.
  • Loan Application Form: Completed loan application with personal and financial details.
  • Additional Documents: Proof of existing debts or collateral (if applicable).

Minimum and Maximum Loan Amount

  • Minimum Amount: Around ₹25,000 to ₹50,000
  • Maximum Amount: Typically ₹10 lakh to ₹50 lakh or more, depending on the lender and the borrower's eligibility.

Interest Rates

The interest rate for a personal loan in India can vary based on several factors, including the lender, the borrower's credit profile, and loan terms. Here are the general ranges for personal loan interest rates:

  • 1. Public Sector Banks:
    Interest Rate Range: Around 9.5% to 14% per annum.
  • 2. Private Sector Banks:
    Interest Rate Range: Around 10.5% to 20% per annum.

Tenure for Personal Loans

The tenure for a personal loan typically varies depending on the lender and the borrower's financial profile. However, common loan tenures for personal loans usually range from:

  • 1. Short-term Loans: 6 months to 2 years:
    These loans typically have higher monthly payments, but the total interest paid is lower.
  • 2. Medium-term Loans: 3 to 5 years:
    A common tenure for personal loans, balancing affordable monthly payments with a moderate amount of interest.
  • 3. Long-term Loans: 6 to 7 years:
    Offers lower monthly payments but results in higher total interest costs over the life of the loan.

Loan tenure options may vary based on the lender, the loan amount, and your creditworthiness. Generally, shorter tenures come with higher monthly payments but less interest paid overall, while longer tenures offer lower monthly payments but may cost more in interest.
