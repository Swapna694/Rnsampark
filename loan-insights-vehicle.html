Vehicle Loan Knowledge Section

A Guide to Understanding Vehicle Loans

What is a Vehicle Loan?

A vehicle loan in banking terms is a type of secured loan provided by banks, financial institutions, or non-banking financial companies (NBFCs) to individuals or businesses for purchasing a vehicle. The vehicle itself acts as collateral for the loan, meaning the lender has the right to seize the vehicle if the borrower fails to repay the loan.

Conditions for Vehicle Loan

  • Age: 21 to 65 years.
  • Income: Stable income (salary or business).
  • Job Type: Employed (salaried or self-employed).
  • Credit Score: Good credit score.
  • Nationality: Indian citizen or resident.
  • Vehicle Type: Loan available for both new and used vehicles.
  • Down Payment: You need to pay a portion of the vehicle price upfront.
  • Existing Debts: Ability to manage current loans/debts.
  • Co-applicant: May be needed if you don’t meet all criteria.
  • Residence Stability: Preference for stable living situation.

Required Documents for Vehicle Loan

  • ID Proof: Aadhar card, Passport, Voter ID, etc.
  • Address Proof: Utility bill, Rent agreement, Passport, etc.
  • Income Proof: Salary slips (for salaried) or Income Tax returns (for self-employed).
  • Photographs: 2-3 passport-sized photos.
  • Bank Statements: Last 3-6 months of bank statements.
  • Vehicle Invoice: Invoice for new car or RC (Registration Certificate) for used car.

Minimum and Maximum Amount for Vehicle Loan

  • Minimum Loan Amount: Typically, the minimum loan amount is around ₹1 lakh to ₹1.5 lakh for a vehicle loan.
  • Maximum Loan Amount: For a new vehicle, lenders usually provide a loan amount of up to 80-90% of the vehicle's on-road price. The maximum loan amount can vary, but it can go up to ₹1 crore or more for high-end cars or premium vehicles, depending on the lender's policies. For used vehicles, the loan amount is generally lower (usually up to 70-80% of the vehicle's valuation).

Interest Rates

The interest rates on vehicle loans may vary based on the loan amount, tenure, and the applicant’s eligibility:

  • New Cars: Typically 8% to 12% per annum.
  • Used Cars: Usually a little higher, around 10% to 16% per annum.
  • Two-Wheeler Loans: Interest rates can range from 10% to 18% per annum, depending on the lender.

Tenure for Vehicle Loans

Vehicle loan tenures vary depending on the loan amount:

  • Minimum: 1 year (12 months)
  • Maximum: 7 years (84 months)
  • General Breakdown:
    New Vehicles: Lenders usually offer loan tenures from 1 to 7 years.
    Used Vehicles: Tenure might be shorter, usually between 1 to 5 years.
  • Choosing Tenure:
    Shorter Tenure: Higher monthly EMI but lower total interest paid.
    Longer Tenure: Lower monthly EMI but higher total interest paid over the life of the loan.
