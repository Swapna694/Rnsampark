Business Loan and Home Loan Knowledge Section

A Guide to Understanding Loans

What is a Business Loan?

A business loan is a financial product provided by a bank or lender to help businesses cover expenses, invest in growth, or manage cash flow, typically repaid with interest over time.

Conditions for Business Loan

  • Self-employed individuals, proprietors, private IT companies, and partnership firms: Eligible for loans based on business type.
  • Business experience: A minimum of 3 years in the current business, with 5 years of total business experience.
  • Profitability: Business must be profit-making for at least the previous 2 years.
  • Age requirement: Applicants should be between 21 and 65 years of age at the time of application and loan maturity.

Required Documents for Business Loan

  • Proof of identity: PAN Card/Driving License/Passport/Voter's ID/Aadhaar Card.
  • Proof of address: Passport/Driving License/Voter's ID/Aadhaar Card/Utility Bill/Bank Statement.
  • Proof of income: Last 3 months' salary slips.
  • Ownership proof: Agreement Copy/Electricity Bill/Share Certificates.
  • Business continuity proof: Shop and Establishment certificate/Tax registrations.
  • Firm constitution: MOA/Partnership Deed/GST Registration Certificate.

Loan Amounts for Business Loan

  • Minimum Loan Amount: ₹40,000 to ₹75,000 for microloans or ₹4 lakh to ₹7 lakh for traditional loans.
  • Maximum Loan Amount: ₹40 lakh to ₹75 crore or more, depending on business qualifications.

Business Loan Interest Rates

  • Traditional Bank Loans: Typically range from 8% to 15% per annum.
  • SBA Loans: Generally have interest rates from 7% to 10% per annum.
  • Business Line of Credit: Interest rates generally range from 10% to 25% per annum.

Tenure for Business Loans

  • Term Loans: 1 to 10 years, depending on the loan amount and business type.
  • SBA Loans: 5 to 25 years, with longer terms for real estate or large loans.
  • Short-Term Loans: 6 months to 3 years, designed for quick funding.
  • Business Line of Credit: 1 to 5 years, with renewal options.
  • Equipment Financing: 1 to 7 years, tied to the life of the equipment.
  • Working Capital Loans: 6 months to 3 years, designed for short-term needs.

What is a Home Loan?

A home loan refers to a financial product offered by banks or financial institutions to help individuals purchase or build a home. The property being bought or constructed serves as collateral for the loan.

Conditions for Home Loan

  • Salaried Individuals: Eligible for home loans if they have a stable job.
  • Professionals (Doctors, Lawyers, etc.): Eligible for loans, with age limits between 21 and 65 years.
  • Self-Employed Individuals: Must demonstrate strong financial records, with age limits of 21 to 65 years.
  • General Eligibility: Must demonstrate financial stability and the ability to repay the loan.

Required Documents for Home Loan

  • Proof of identity: PAN card, passport, voter ID, or Aadhaar card.
  • Proof of address: Utility bills, Aadhaar, or passport.
  • Proof of income: Last 3 months salary slips.
  • Self-employed: Income tax returns (ITR), bank statements.
  • Property documents: Sale agreement, title deed, approved building plan.
  • Employment details: Employment certificate, income tax returns.

Minimum Amount and Margin for Home Loan

  • Minimum Loan Amount: Starts at ₹3 lakh.
  • Margin for Home Loans: Loans up to ₹7.3 lakh require a 10% margin, higher loans require a 20% to 25% margin.

Home Loan Interest Rates

  • Fixed Interest Rates: Typically 8% to 10%.
  • Floating Interest Rates: Typically 7% to 9%, subject to market conditions.
  • Special Rates: Some banks offer discounts for women or first-time buyers.
  • Credit Score-Based Rates: Higher credit scores get better rates.

Home Loan Tenures

  • 5 to 10 years: Shorter tenures, higher EMIs, less interest paid.
  • 10 to 15 years: A balance of affordable EMIs and faster repayment.
  • 15 to 20 years: Common tenure, offering reasonable EMIs and manageable interest.
  • 20 to 30 years: Lower EMIs but higher total interest.
