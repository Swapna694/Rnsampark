Frequently Asked Questions

Can I change my EMI amount after the loan is approved? ‚û§

Yes, it is possible to adjust your EMI amount, depending on the lender's policy. However, changing the EMI might affect the loan tenure or the total interest paid. You would need to contact your lender and apply for a revision of the loan terms, including a possible change in EMI.

How does EMI affect my credit score? ‚û§

Your credit score can be positively or negatively affected by how consistently you pay your EMIs. Timely EMI payments improve your credit score, while missed or delayed payments can lower it.

What happens if I miss an EMI payment? ‚û§

If you miss an EMI payment, your lender may charge a penalty, and your credit score may be impacted negatively. In extreme cases, missed payments can lead to loan default and legal consequences. It's important to communicate with your lender if you're facing any difficulties in making payments.
